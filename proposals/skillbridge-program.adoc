== Internships

=== Georgia Cyber Range

include::../templates/gcr_intro.adoc[]

Internship opportunities are available for many career alignments to include: computer scientists, electrical engineers, mathematicians, cryptologists, and more.
Requisite skills and interests are closely aligned with 17-series (Cyberspace Operations) MOS's and AOC's.

Qualified service members can expect to spend the majority of their time working alongside talented developers on the Georgia Cyber Range.
The languages/frameworks/DSLs used in the Georgia Cyber Range include:

* Python
* Salt/Jinja
* C
* Javascript/CSS/HTML

Functional knowledge in one or more of these specific languages/key areas specified is a requirement for applicants. Service member's active contributions are pushed to upstream open-source projects that power the Georgia Cyber Range. The contributions will become a permanent fixture in the respective project(s).
The work of service members, if accepted upstream, will be pulled down and improve the suite of capabilities offered by the Georgia Cyber Range.

In addition to traditional programming, service members are provided unique opportunities to work with specific technologies that include:

* Distributed system optimization related to the implementation and tuning of census algorithms (Paxos, RAFT, CRUSH, and others).
* Software-defined networking pushing layer 3 topologies to hosts as an underlay network.

==== Interviews

Service members that complete a program are granted an interview with one of the partners located at the Georgia Cyber Center. The interview directly relates to the work performed during the internship.

=== Executive Director Shadow Program
The Executive Director Shadow Program is specifically designed to provide senior service members with executive leadership experience in a complex and dynamic organization outside of their normal military experience.  The unique ecosystem of the Georgia Cyber Center provides senior service members with an opportunity to interact directly and routinely with local, state, and federal government, academia, and private sector entities that are focused on cyber operations and cybersecurity.  In addition to gaining exposure to senior leaders in both the public and private sector, service members will gain specific experience in Business Development, Program Management, Grants Management, Innovation Management, Organizational Strategy, and Organizational Leadership.

=== Augusta University Cybersecurity Operations Center 

In October 2020, the new Augusta University Cybersecurity Operations Center will open in the Georgia Cyber Center.  The mission of the Cybersecurity Operations Center is to protect both the Augusta University and Augusta University Health System networks and systems, providing monitoring, security solutions, incident response, threat hunting, and Governance/Risk/Compliance (GRC) services.  Plans include providing SOC-as-a-Service to outside entities as the Cybersecurity Operations Center services mature.  Additionally, to ensure that Augusta University faculty and students can utilize innovative technology to improve the learning experience both onsite and at a distance, the Augusta University Division of Information Technology operates an Audio/Visual System Engineering team that designs, installs, and commissions state of the art technology enhancements in classrooms and meeting spaces per ANSI/AVIXA standards.

*Types of positions:*

*   SOC (Cyber Defense) Analyst
*   Cyber Security Engineers
*   GRC Analysts
*   GRC Engineers
*   Audiovisual Systems Engineer
*   Audiovisual Technician/Installers
*   Director of Audiovisual and Event Services

== Certifications and Training

=== Cyber Workforce Academy

The Cyber Workforce Academy focuses on cybersecurity training, awareness, and outreach to learners at all levels providing relevant and affordable training to information technology and cybersecurity professionals.

In collaboration with the Fort Gordon SFL-Tap, under the Career Skill Program, the Workforce Academy created several tracks for transitioning service members to develop, enhance, and acquire skills in the cybersecurity industry.

Each track provides service members customized industry training that leads to highly recognized industry certifications with field exposure to widen future career opportunities.

=== Program Tracks

==== Beginner
The beginner track is designed for service members with no prior training or experience in the IT and cybersecurity field. 
Completion of the beginner track provides service members with the knowledge to obtain the following top industry certifications:

*   CompTIA A+ +
    _The starting point certification for a career in IT. The performance-based exams certify foundational IT skills across a variety of devices and operating systems._
*   CompTIA Network+ +
    _Industry credential that certifies the essential skills needed to confidently design, configure, manage, and troubleshoot any wired and wireless device._
*   CompTIA Security+ +
    _Industry recognized certification that provides a global benchmark for best practices in IT network and operational security._

The total duration of the beginner program is 6 months. Each course is self paced (up to 8 weeks alloted for completion) and tailored to the listed certifications.
During the program, service members train for certifications and simultaneously apply newly obtained skills through industry field experience in an area of IT or cybersecurity.

*Soldier Expense*

*   Training bundle price:  $2,297
*   A+ Training is priced at $899
*   Network+ and Security+ training is priced at $699

NOTE: Service members can utilize Credential Assistance, Tuition Assistance, or VA benefits to cover the training costs.

====  Intermediate

===== Network Engineer Track
The Network Engineer Track is designed for service members with intermediate IT and/or cybersecurity experience.
Requisite skills and interests are closely aligned with 25S, 25U, 25Q, and 25M, 25D, 35T, 35Q, 35N MOS's.
Completion of the Network Engineer Track provides service members with the knowledge to obtain the following top industry certifications:

*   CompTIA Network+ +
    _Industry credential that certifies the essential skills needed to confidently design, configure, manage, and troubleshoot any wired and wireless device._
*   Cisco CCNA +
    _Industry certification that validates skills and knowledge in network fundamentals, network access, IP connectivity, IP services, security fundamentals, automation and programming._

During the program (Network+ 8 weeks, CCNA 16 weeks), service members train for certifications and simultaneously apply advanced skills while working with industry partners.

*Soldier Expense*

*   Training bundle price: $6,999
*   Network+ training is priced at $699
*   Cisco CCNA training is priced at $6,300

NOTE: Service members can utilize Credential Assistance, Tuition Assistance, or VA benefits to cover the training costs.

===== Security Operations Center (SOC) Track
The Security Operations Center Track is designed for service members with intermediate IT and/or cybersecurity experience that seek a career in a security operations center or cybersecurity operations.
Requisite skills and interests are closely aligned with Signal Corp 25N, 25B, 25S, 25T, 25P, and 25Z, 25D, 35Q, 35N, 35S, 35T, 17C MOS's.
Completion of the SOC Track provides service members with the knowledge to obtain the following top industry certifications:

* CompTIA Network+ +
    _Industry credential that certifies the essential skills needed to confidently design, configure, manage, and troubleshoot any wired and wireless device._
* CompTIA Security+ +
    _Industry recognized certification that provides a global benchmark for best practices in IT network and operational security._
* Cisco Cyber Ops +
    _Industry recognized certification that prepares candidates to begin a career working with associate-level cybersecurity analysts within security operations centers._

The total duration of the SOC track is 6 months (each course is 8 weeks). During the program, service members train for certifications and simultaneously apply advanced skills while working in the Augusta University Security Operations Center or Georgia Cyber Range.


*Soldier Expense*

*   Training bundle price: Total cost $3,593
*   Network+ and Security+ training is priced at $699
*   Cisco CyberOps training is priced at $2,195
*   All training packages include exam vouchers and training material.

NOTE: Service members can utilize Credential Assistance, Tuition Assistance, or VA benefits to cover the training costs.
